Trust, is defined by Merriam Webster as “the body of real things, events, and facts”. This is a crude synopsis of it's intricate layers: trust is strong but delicate, safe but dangerous, virtuous but evil. It can destroy relationships but be the foundation for new ones. Nobody can truly define such an enigmatic term, yet we see it everywhere. It’s hammered into our children at a young age through folklore, and the message is repeated in movies and dramas. Everybody knows a truthful society is superior to a deceitful one. However, few are able to articulate the complete purview under the philosophy of which trust resides. Media frames trust as boiled down to moral obligation when in fact you are the main beneficiary.
It’s important to ascertain lying from not telling the truth. Lying is not just the statement of something false, but also the withholding of information. Discreetly stealing a cookie would constitute lying, but not fit the parameters of not telling the truth, as nobody yet asked where the cookie went. This is in fact, the most prevalent and harmful type of lie we see today. Lying is a sign of cognitive development in young toddlers; the fermentation of creativity, however, divergence from our childish inclinations is imperative to negate the consequences they bring. Lying is akin to putting on a mask, and the mask thickens with every lie. Thus deceiving others is essentially denying yourself, and disparaging your word. Say a man and a woman get married and settle down. The man cheats in his inebriated state of mind. Destiny then paves two paths he can choose, one of honesty and the other of lies. If he pursues the latter, he could keep this secret for the rest of his life but in the end, he is never truly loved.
The wife only loves his mask, the version of the husband who stayed faithful, not the real deal; a fake, a fraud. This is actually the best-case scenario when choosing a path of lies. Gradually, your mask thickens to a point where the user is isolated, suffocated, trapped in his/her own mind. The weight of their sins fills them to the brim with guilt and torment to such an extent that the body naturally begins to vent. Bottling up their “toxicity” ironically hurts those he’s trying to protect because toxicity emerges in other, more discrete ways. Each lie tethers the user to a fake persona, and it’s stressful to potentially have to uphold that lie in the future. Free your mind from the shackles of deception and you’ll be a lot happier knowing you have nothing to hide. Nevertheless, conviction is perhaps the sweetest perk of radical honesty. Choosing a life of truth gives you more conviction than you will ever need. Gatherings hinge on your every word after choosing a life of truth and exhibiting your adopted philosophy. That is conviction. That is power. That is the truth...
